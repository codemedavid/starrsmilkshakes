## 1. Executive Summary

*   **What is this project about?** (A concise, 1-2 paragraph overview of the project, its purpose, and the primary problem it solves.)
*   **What is the core value proposition?**
*   **What are the expected outcomes?**

---

## 2. Problem Statement

*   **What specific problem are we trying to solve?**
    *   *From whose perspective?* (e.g., users, business, internal teams)
    *   *What are the current pain points or inefficiencies?*
*   **Why is this problem important to solve now?**
    *   *What is the impact of not solving it?* (e.g., lost revenue, customer churn, operational inefficiency)
*   **What evidence or data supports the existence and urgency of this problem?** (e.g., user feedback, market analysis, competitor gaps, internal data)

---

## 3. Vision & Goals

### 3.1 Product Vision (Long-term Aspiration)

*   **What is the ultimate future state we envision for this product/feature in the long run?** (1-2 sentences)

### 3.2 Project Goals (SMART Objectives for this specific project)

*   **What are the measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound objectives for *this phase* or *this project*?**
    *   *Example:* Increase user engagement by X% within Y months.
    *   *Example:* Reduce customer support tickets related to Z by A% within B weeks.
    *   *Example:* Launch feature X to 100% of users by Q3.

---

## 4. Target Audience

*   **Who are the primary users/customers for this product/feature?** (Describe their demographics, behaviors, needs, and motivations.)
*   **Are there any secondary audiences or stakeholders?** (e.g., internal teams, partners)
*   *(Optional: Link to User Personas if applicable)*

---

## 5. Solution Overview

*   **What is the high-level solution we propose to address the problem?** (Describe the core concept and how it will work at a high level, without getting into granular features yet.)
*   **What are the key benefits for the user and the business?**
*   **How does this solution fit into our overall product strategy?**
*   **Are there any existing solutions (internal or external) and how does ours differentiate?**

---

## 6. Scope & MVP Definition

### 6.1 In-Scope Features (for MVP)

*   **What are the essential functionalities required for the Minimum Viable Product (MVP)?** (List high-level features or epics. Avoid getting into detailed user stories here, keep it strategic.)
    *   *Example:* User registration & profile management
    *   *Example:* Basic content creation flow
    *   *Example:* Ability to share content
*   **What defines "viable" for this MVP?** (What is the minimum threshold of value we must deliver to consider this project a success and gather initial feedback?)

### 6.2 Out-of-Scope Features (for future phases)

*   **What features or functionalities are explicitly *not* included in this initial phase but are planned for future iterations?** (This helps manage expectations and prevent scope creep.)
    *   *Example:* Advanced analytics dashboard
    *   *Example:* Integration with third-party tools
    *   *Example:* Mobile app specific features

---

## 7. Functional Requirements / Features (Detailed)

*   **Break down the in-scope features into more detailed functional requirements.** (Often expressed as User Stories, but can also be specific requirements.)
    *   **Feature Group 1: [e.g., User Onboarding]**
        *   *User Story 1:* As a new user, I want to register with my email, so that I can create an account.
        *   *User Story 2:* As a returning user, I want to log in securely, so that I can access my content.
    *   **Feature Group 2: [e.g., Content Creation]**
        *   *User Story 3:* As a creator, I want to upload an image, so that I can share it with my audience.
        *   *User Story 4:* As a creator, I want to add a caption to my image, so that I can provide context.
*   **Prioritization (Optional but recommended):** Indicate priority for each feature/user story (e.g., Must Have, Should Have, Could Have, Won't Have / MoSCoW, or High/Medium/Low).

---

## 8. Non-Functional Requirements

*   **What are the technical and operational constraints or qualities required?**
    *   **Performance:** (e.g., page load times, API response times, number of concurrent users supported)
    *   **Security:** (e.g., data encryption, authentication standards, compliance needs)
    *   **Reliability & Availability:** (e.g., uptime targets, error rates)
    *   **Scalability:** (e.g., ability to handle X growth in users/data over Y period)
    *   **Usability:** (e.g., accessibility standards, learnability)
    *   **Compatibility:** (e.g., browser support, device support)
    *   **Maintainability:** (e.g., ease of debugging, future enhancements)
    *   **Compliance:** (e.g., GDPR, HIPAA, WCAG)

---

## 9. Success Metrics (KPIs)

*   **How will we measure the success of this project against our goals?** (List specific, measurable Key Performance Indicators.)
    *   *Example:* % increase in daily active users (DAU)
    *   *Example:* Average time spent per session
    *   *Example:* Conversion rate from X to Y
    *   *Example:* Net Promoter Score (NPS)
    *   *Example:* Reduction in customer support contacts for specific issues

---

## 10. Technical Considerations & Dependencies

*   **What are the key technical architectural decisions, assumptions, or existing infrastructure that impact this project?**
*   **Are there any specific technologies to be used or avoided?**
*   **What are the internal or external dependencies?** (e.g., reliance on another team's API, third-party services, legacy systems)
*   **Data Model Impact:** (How will this project affect our existing data models? New data needed?)

---

## 11. Assumptions, Constraints & Risks

### 11.1 Assumptions

*   **What are we assuming to be true for this project to be successful?** (e.g., "We assume the API from Partner X will be ready by Date Y," "We assume a certain level of user tech-savviness.")

### 11.2 Constraints

*   **What are the limitations or restrictions on this project?** (e.g., budget, timeline, available resources, technological limitations, regulatory requirements)

### 11.3 Risks & Mitigation

*   **What potential risks could impact the project's success, timeline, or quality?**
    *   *Risk 1:* [Description of risk]
        *   *Mitigation:* [Plan to reduce or manage the risk]
    *   *Risk 2:* [Description of risk]
        *   *Mitigation:* [Plan to reduce or manage the risk]
